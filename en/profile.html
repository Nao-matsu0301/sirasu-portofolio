    • Who is Osame Sirasu？

    Occupational therapist students × Artist

    Childhood

    I was active in karate, soccer, and tree climbing, and was not very interested in art. In junior high school, I was bullied for painting with paint on my hands during art class, which led to being frequently absent from school.

    COVID-19 Disaster

    In the winter of my sophomore year of high school, my parents' finances began to suffer as a result of a series of cancellations of major events, including the cancellation of a prefectural tournament for my club's activities. Not wanting to put too much of a burden on my parents, she began to make a living by creating game icons for my friends' games.

    Tranition

    At the time of my higher education, I started studying to become a physical therapist. I found an occupational therapist in an article about end-of-life care who changed my future. The occupational therapist held an exhibition featuring the artwork of dead people.

    Now and Future

    Moved by the warmth with which her bereaved family and friends were embraced by the exhibition of her posthumous works, I decided that I help many people through art. Currently, as a medical x artist, I am attending a vocational school to become a licensed occupational therapist, creating many works as an artist and participating in events.
